Teaching & Mentoring

Teaching has been one of the most fun and rewarding opportunities of my career. I have taught seven different courses between Dartmouth College and Colby-Sawyer College, ranging from introductory classes such as Introduction to Environmental Science and Anatomy and Physiology, to intermediate and advanced courses such as Field Methods in Ecology, Physiological Ecology, and Aquatic Ecology. I love bringing students into the field to observe natural processes firsthand and to get hands-on practice with diverse methods and approaches. I have had the great plasure of teaching and mentoring high school students from Greenland, Denmark, and the US through the NSF IGERT (Integrative Graduate Education and Research Traineeship) and JSEP (Joint Science Education Program) programs in Kangerlussuaq, Greenland. I have also mentored seven undergraduate student research projects and I am always looking for enthusiastic students to assist me with research in the lab and in the field!



Outreach

I strongly believe that science benefits from diverse perspectives and contributions from the entire community. I enjoy communicating science to non-scientific audiences and bringing my research to community events, meetings, and classrooms of all ages. I have also been active in helping train water quality managers and community scientists in methods for lake monitoring through the Cyanobacterial Monitoring Collaborative (CMC). I have also been working with the Lake Sunapee Protective Association (LSPA) to develop a summer cyanobacteria monitoring program that trains community members how to collect, count, and identify key cyanobacteria species in the lake using customized kits. In addition, the community scientists sample on days with known satellite overpasses to enable these data to be paired with satellite imagery and aid in the development of methods for remotely sensing cyanobacterial conditions in the lake.
