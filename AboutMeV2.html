About Me

I am an interdisciplinary aquatic ecologist and I am currently a postdoc in Environmental Studies at Dartmouth College. I enjoy studying freshwater ecosystems through diverse approaches. I use a wide range of tools from different disciplines, including ecology, toxicology, and remote sensing, to investigate ecological questions related to lake water quality, cyanobacteria, and aquatic food webs. A central theme of my research is understanding how cyanobacteria and their toxins influence freshwater food webs, as well as the surrounding terrestrial landscape. In addition to studying lakes in the northeast, some of my research addresses these questions in polar ecosystems, where lakes and ponds are experiencing climate-related changes such as shifts in ice cover duration, precipitation, and melting permafrost.
